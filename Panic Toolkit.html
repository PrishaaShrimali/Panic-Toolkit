When Things Feel Dark

Read slowly. You don't have to believe all of this right now. Just let it be here with you.

🌊 This feeling is not permanent.

Every low you've ever felt has eventually lifted — even the ones that seemed like they never would. Your nervous system is in overdrive right now, not broken. This exact moment will pass.

You have survived 100% of your worst days so far.

🧠 Your brain is lying to you right now.

When you're in a low, your brain offers you evidence that things are hopeless, that you're not enough, that this is just how it will always be. That is the depression speaking — not the truth.

"I don't even believe the kind things anymore."

You wrote that. You knew it wasn't right. That awareness — even when it hurts — is your intelligence working for you, not against you.

📚 You are more than your grades.

The version of you that exists when no one is grading anything — the one who writes poems at midnight, who teaches middle schoolers, who takes the bus just to feel the motion — that person is real and worth knowing.

Your worth is not a GPA. It never was.

🐶 Things worth staying for.

The golden retriever you're going to have someday. The summer of travel you're going to take. The places you haven't been yet. The version of you that gets to exist on the other side of this.

The college acceptance letters you're going to read. The things you're going to build. The poems you haven't written yet.

You have things ahead of you worth seeing.

💚 If it's too much to hold alone

You don't have to figure this out by yourself. Reaching out is not weakness — it's the hardest and bravest thing.
